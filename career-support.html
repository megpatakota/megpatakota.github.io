What we sort

The useful work is usually not the obvious work.

A CV review might be part of it, but only after we know what the CV needs to prove. The point is to find the decision that will make the next move less noisy.

  • Role map

    Which role families fit your current evidence, which ones need a bridge, and which ones are wasting your energy right now.

  • Evidence story

    How to make a degree, dissertation, project, internship, job, or side experiment sound relevant without pretending it was bigger than it was.

  • Process read

    What a company may be testing through a screen, take-home task, case, technical round, or hiring-manager conversation.

  • Next seven days

    A short plan: what to apply for, what to rewrite, what to practise, who to contact, and what to stop over-fixing.

Why Meg

I read the market through real interview patterns.

I keep a private interview and application record with role titles, salary bands, team shapes, office expectations, interview rounds, take-home tasks, and questions that reveal what companies are really looking for. I do not share private details. I use the patterns to ask better questions.
